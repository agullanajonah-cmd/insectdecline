Understanding the Issue

One of the least-discussed but most pressing environmental issues today is the global decline of insect populations. Insects may be small, but they are foundational to the planet‚Äôs ecosystems.

According to El-Shafie (2022) Insect decline is a serious threat because their abundance, number, diversity and the extinction of whole species. Decline in population does not mean only reduction in the insect numbers but also means the restricted geographical distribution which leads to the first step of extinction.

They have adapted to diverse habitats, feeding on a wide range of substances and establishing ecological niches that make them vital for ecosystem balance.

Yet, this crucial group of organisms is in serious decline. As Pena (2022) stated that insects are disappearing. Fireflies have not been in sight for a long time. In addition to that, Pena also stated that dragonflies, grasshoppers, and butterflies sightings have been fewer than before.

The decline of insect populations therefore threatens not just biodiversity, but the very systems that support life on Earth.

Nearly half of all insect species worldwide are in rapid decline and a third could disappear altogether, according to a study warning of dire consequences for crop pollination and natural food chains. (France-Presse, 2019)
